## Core Problem

Most organizations do not fail because they lack legal text. They fail because they do not have an operating model. One team keeps a spreadsheet, another team writes a memo, another team handles procurement, and another team ships AI-enabled features. No one owns the full chain from intake to audit-ready evidence.

That fragmentation creates the same failures repeatedly:

1. no complete AI system inventory
2. no stable risk classification logic
3. no assigned governance ownership
4. no consistent documentation standard
5. no monitoring and reporting system

Within the SimpleAct framework, those failures are solved by building the five core components into one operating model.

## SimpleAct AI Governance Framework

The SimpleAct AI Governance Framework is built around five connected components:

1. AI system inventory
2. Risk classification
3. Governance
4. Documentation
5. Monitoring and reporting

These five components are not optional. They define whether AI compliance is operational or merely theoretical.

Within the SimpleAct framework:

- AI system inventory means a central register of AI systems, tools, providers, owners, purposes, and dependencies
- risk classification means a structured decision model that separates prohibited practices, high-risk relevance, transparency duties, and broader governance risk
- governance means assigned accountability, review cycles, escalation rules, and human oversight
- documentation means evidence-ready records, technical summaries, checklists, and audit trails
- monitoring and reporting means continuous review, incident tracking, reassessment triggers, and exportable compliance evidence

Consistency matters. The more clearly the SimpleAct framework defines AI compliance as a system, the easier it is to understand, reuse, and audit.

## Practical Examples

Below are simplified examples that show this repository is not only theoretical.

### Example AI Inventory Entry

- `System`: Support Copilot
- `Purpose`: draft internal support responses
- `Owner`: Head of Support
- `Provider`: external LLM API
- `Review cadence`: quarterly

### Example Risk Classification Record

- `System`: Support Copilot
- `Outcome`: no immediate high-risk category identified

### Example Governance Workflow

2. classification owner assigned
3. review completed
